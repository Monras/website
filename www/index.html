Who we are
ÆSIR consists of 33 students from KTH Royal Institute of Technology, in Stockholm. We are spread over several programmes and degrees - including Engineering Physics, Electrical Engineering and Aerospace Engineering. Our team is multicultural, with over 12 nations represented.

Our teams
Aerodynamics
The Aerodynamics team is responsible for the design of the aerodynamics of our rockets. This includes the nose-cone, fuselage, control surfaces and parachutes.
They are also working with rocket stabilisation using the control surfaces, trajectory predictions and parachute design. Their goal is to make sure the rocket flies straight and comes down safe.
Mechanical
The Mechanical team is responsible for designing and manufacturing our ground equipment. This includes a vertical test stand for a 3kN hybrid rocket engine and a launch rail.
They are the ones who manufacture the rocket body and integrate engine, electronics and parachute system into it.
Propulsion
The Propulsion team is responsible for the development and validation of our rocket engines.
Their first project is to integrate an imported rocket motor with the work of the aerodynamics team. Their goal is to develop and manufacture our future and current rocket engines.
Systems
The Systems team works with the design and development of the rockets electronics and communication system.
They make sure the rocket knows its trajectory, speed, height, location and when to eject its parachute. They are also responsible for all the ground equipment necessary for communication. Their main goal is to make sure we find the rocket after launch.
PR
The PR team take care of our public relations. They work with graphical design, video, photography and outreach in social media, as well as on our website.
They are also responsible for coordinating contacts with sponsors and partners, as well as arranging our participation in fairs. Their goal is to make the association known to potential new members and to acquire the resources needed to complete our projects.
Events
The Events team arrange internal social activities like parties and BBQs, but also outreach activities like lectures and meetups.
Their work go hand in hand with the work of the PR team, but is more focused on the events where recruitment to ÆSIR happens and spreading the knowledge we gain. Their goal is to make the team members have fun while learning together.
Our mission
ÆSIR was created to provide a forum for students with an interest in rocket propulsion, where they can develop their passion. The activities of the association will further strengthen KTH position as a leading Aerospace University. Our mission is to become one of the top student organizations for rocket building in the world, by breaking and holding the student altitude records (which is currently 21.5 kilometers). Ultimately, we intend to break the worldwide altitude record and reach altitudes beyond the 120 kilometer mark.
Why support us
By sponsoring ÆSIR, you get the chance to both get your name out there through logo placement on our rockets and to support a group of students in breaking the European record of altitude for student rocketry. We gladly accept any kind of sponsoring - funding, equipment or knowledge - anything helps our cause.
